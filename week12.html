Week 12

Advancing Testing with Playwright and Jest,
Finalizing CMS UI

April 21 - 25, 2025

In the twelfth week of our training at StraStan Solutions Corps, our focus remained on refining and strengthening the quality assurance process as the project neared its final stages. We participated in our regular weekly scrum meeting, where we discussed testing progress, current challenges, and final development goals. These scrums have been instrumental in keeping the team aligned and identifying areas that need further attention as we approach completion.

A major part of our focus this week was automated testing using Playwright, which we have been utilizing over the past few weeks to simulate real user interactions and validate the system’s behavior across different scenarios. By this point, we’ve grown more familiar with the tool and have developed test scripts that cover both core functionality and edge cases. This has allowed us to detect subtle issues early and confirm that previous bugs remain resolved after backend updates.

Additionally, we began exploring Jest, a JavaScript testing framework commonly used for unit testing. While Playwright focuses on end-to-end user interactions, Jest allows us to test specific logic and components within the system. Starting with Jest has broadened our understanding of the testing stack, giving us a more complete picture of how different testing layers contribute to system reliability. This dual approach—using both Playwright for UI-level testing and Jest for functional/unit testing—has significantly improved our testing coverage.

Another important milestone this week was finalizing the CMS (Content Management System) and its user interface (UI). As testers, we provided feedback on the CMS’s design, layout, and usability, ensuring that the final version is both functional and intuitive. We checked that content creation, organization, and updates are handled smoothly through the CMS and that the UI meets accessibility and design standards. This part of the work also emphasized the importance of aligning development with user experience (UX) best practices.

Overall, Week 12 represented a blend of deeper technical work and system refinement. With testing tools like Playwright and Jest now integrated into our workflow and the CMS nearing completion, we are in a strong position to transition into the final testing phase and prepare for deployment. The experience has enhanced our understanding of automated and unit testing, as well as the importance of collaborative validation during product finalization.
