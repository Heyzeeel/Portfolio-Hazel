Week 13

Consistent Testing Amid a Shortened Workweek

April 28 - May 2, 2025

In the eleventh week of our training at StraStan Solutions Corps, we continued focusing on both manual and automation testing, maintaining our quality assurance efforts despite a shortened workweek due to the observance of Holy Week. With only three working days available, our team remained consistent and efficient, ensuring that our testing progress would not be significantly affected.

During this time, we followed the same structure and workflow established in previous weeks. Our manual testing approach involved simulating various user interactions, identifying potential bugs, and verifying that the system met all functional requirements. This hands-on process remained essential in catching interface inconsistencies and logical errors that automated tools might not detect.

Simultaneously, we continued leveraging Playwright for automation testing, running test scripts, refining scenarios, and validating system behaviors in bulk. Automation allowed us to maintain testing coverage across core features despite the limited working hours. It also ensured that regression testing could be performed quickly and consistently as backend updates were pushed throughout the week.

Although this week was lighter in terms of hours, it still contributed meaningfully to the overall testing phase. We took advantage of the available time to stay aligned with our tasks and made sure that any issues discovered were documented and relayed to the development team for resolution.

This week also reinforced the value of having a structured and repeatable testing process, especially when dealing with shorter timelines. By maintaining discipline and consistency, we were able to continue making steady progress toward a stable and reliable system, even with fewer working days. As we enter the final stretch of our internship, our focus remains on completing the remaining test cases, supporting development as needed, and ensuring that the system is ready for its final release with minimal issues.
