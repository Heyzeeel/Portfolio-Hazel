Week 08

Initiating Manual Testing and
Weekly Progress Discussions

March 24 - 28, 2025

In the eighth week of our training at StraStan Solutions Corps, Trina and I officially began the manual testing phase for the system. As testers, our primary objective was to evaluate the systemâ€™s functionality, ensuring that all features worked as expected before deployment. This required a structured approach to systematically test different components, identify potential issues, and document our findings for future improvements.

To organize our testing process efficiently, we utilized Notion as our primary tool for tracking test cases. We created a comprehensive list of features to be tested, breaking them down into sections and sub-sections. This structured approach allowed us to methodically examine each functionality, ensuring thorough validation of all aspects of the system. By segmenting our testing into smaller, manageable parts, we could focus on specific components while maintaining a clear overview of the overall system performance.

The manual testing phase involved simulating real-world scenarios, executing various user interactions, and observing how the system responded. We carefully documented any discrepancies, bugs, or unexpected behaviors encountered during testing. Our goal was to provide detailed and actionable feedback to the development team, allowing them to address any issues before the final deployment.

To keep track of our progress and align our efforts with the rest of the team, we conducted a weekly meeting where we discussed our accomplishments, challenges, and next steps. These meetings allowed us to present our findings, share insights, and coordinate with other team members working on different aspects of the project. By regularly reviewing our work, we ensured that testing remained on schedule and that necessary adjustments could be made based on new developments.

This week provided valuable insights into quality assurance (QA) processes, emphasizing the importance of systematic testing in software development. Through hands-on testing, we strengthened our ability to identify potential flaws and gained a deeper understanding of how different system components interact.

Looking ahead to the ninth week, we are considering automated testing as the next phase of our QA process. This would involve using testing tools and browser extensions that are new to us, allowing us to gain additional experience in automated testing methodologies. Implementing automation will help improve efficiency, increase test coverage, and minimize human error, ultimately contributing to a more robust and reliable system.

As we progress, our focus will be on completing the remaining test cases, reporting findings, and collaborating with the development team to implement necessary fixes. This phase of the project is crucial in delivering a seamless and functional system that meets user expectations and business requirements.
